Module 3 - Unit 2 Artifacts

Seminar 1 blog post

Some say that people are the biggest risk of cyber security. Select five terms from ISO/IEC Standard 27000 Section 3 Terms and Definitions and write a 300-word blog post on how people can be managed to overcome cyber security attacks from the inside. There will also be an opportunity to review your team’s progress during the seminar.

Audit

As defined in ISO/IEC Standard 27000 (ISO, 2021), Audit is a systematic, independent and documented process of determining the extent to which the audit criteria are fulfilled by obtaining audit evidence and evaluating. An audit can be an internal, external or combined audit. Managing people to conduct GDPR audits could be important in determining whether policies and procedures are adequate to regulate the processing of personal data.

Access Control

Access Control ensures that access to assets is authorized and restricted based on business and security requirements (ISO, 2021). In other words, only the intended people can access security classified data. Access Control thus serves as a fundamental building block for secure information sharing (Bertino, E., Ghinita, G. & Kamra, A., 2011). Role Based Access Control, which is a popular access control model, can be achieved by defining the employees’ roles when processing data. Assigning permissions to roles instead of individuals makes the implementation of access control flexible (Healey, R., 2021). Besides, Principle of Least Privilege (POLP) ensures users are also only given the minimum level of access which is required to finish their tasks.

Authentication

In addition to access control, authentication is also of paramount importance working along with the access control. Authentication is the provision of assurance that a claimed characteristic of an entity is correct (ISO, 2021). Two-factor authentication is one way of doing authentication which also complies with GDPR (Melech, D. B., 2018).

Management System

A management system is a set of interrelated or interacting elements of an organization to establish policies and objectives and processes to achieve those objectives (ISO, 2021). Organization’s structure, roles and responsibilities should be carefully designed with high priority concerns of security. It is also suggested that responsibility of security implementation should not only be the responsibility of the software security team, but all the developers.

Residual Risk

Residual risk is the risk remaining after risk treatment (ISO, 2021), while inherent risk is the risk that was faced by the organization before any security countermeasures were implemented. It is possible that the security implementation cannot prevent the targeted risk thoroughly, or sometimes security teams may just focus only on the inherent risk but are missing the full picture (Fasulo, P. 2020). Therefore, all staff, as mentioned in the above paragraph about the word management, should also be responsible and be alerted on possible risk despite prevention measures are already in place.
