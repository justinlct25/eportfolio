Exploring the Cyclomatic Complexity's Relevance Today

The Cyclomatic Complexity is commonly considered in modules on testing the validity of code design today. However, in your opinion, should it be? Does it remain relevant today? Specific to the focus of this module, is it relevant in our quest to develop secure software? Justify all opinions which support your argument and share your responses with your team.

According to Wade (2021), Cyclomatic Complexity is a measure of how “complex” a piece of code is through counting the number of unique paths users could take to reach the end or hit all combinations of decision points. It is a measure of the logical complexity of a module and the minimum effort necessary to qualify a module(McCabe, 2010). The Cyclomatic Complexity may also be considered as the numbers of unit tests have to be written to test all possible outcomes of a unique method.

As the study conducted by Alenezi and Zarour (2020) shows that, the higher the complexity of a software or system is, the greater chances of security flaws or bugs are. High complexity increases the difficulty for the developers to understand the software, thus the maintainability of the system is reduced. High Cyclomatic Complexity also makes it harder for writing test cases to cover all possible conditions. With considering the Cyclomatic Complexity while developing, the occurrence of dirty code can be reduced.

However, we also found that there are limitations for Cyclomatic Complexity. Complexity such as the complexity of the data or conditions like nested conditional structures are difficult to be included in the Cyclomatic Complexity (akash1295, 2021). In contrast, some simple decision structures and comparisons may result in a misleading figure. Also, some codes are complex by nature, thus strictly sticking to the Cyclomatic Complexity threshold or limits may hinder the target functioning of the module.

Cyclomatic Complexity could be a good metric indicating the complexity and maintainability which affect the security of the software. However, considering the limitations mentioned above it should not be the only indicator that software developers depend on. Software developers should always be aware of security concerns and start planning and implementing security related designs in the early stages.

Reference:

  • Alenezi, Mamdouh & Zarour, Mohammad. (2020). On the Relationship between Software Complexity and Security.
