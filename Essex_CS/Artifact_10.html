What is an Ontology?

What do you understand about the ontology that has been presented for your reading this week?

Before talking about Ontology, Service-oriented architectures (SOA) were the architectures which evolved from the existence of monolithic applications and distributed systems. Clients interact with servers and make use of the modules of functionality available. The modules are loosely coupled with each other, easing their deployability and use. Since they are implemented independently, they are not dependent on a specific platform or software.

Ontology is one of the four SOA standards. According to Open Group and the lecturecast of this week, Ontology is a hierarchy of relevant terms for a system, which captures the relationships between the elements. It is defined using the Web Ontology Language (OWL). Ontologies can be used to enhance understanding, organise repositories and support communications between technical and business people.

Could you attempt to define an ontology that would be relevant to the system that you are designing for the summative assessment?
